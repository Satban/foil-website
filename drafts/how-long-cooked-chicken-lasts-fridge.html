How Long Does Cooked Chicken Last in the Fridge?

Cooked chicken can last in the fridge for 3 to 4 days when stored correctly. After that, it’s best to toss it to avoid any health risks.

The Details

To maximize the freshness of your cooked chicken, store it in an airtight container or wrap it tightly with plastic wrap or aluminum foil. Make sure your fridge is set at 40°F (4°C) or below to keep bacteria at bay. If you notice that you won’t eat the cooked chicken within the 3 to 4-day window, consider freezing it instead—cooked chicken can be kept in the freezer for about 2 to 6 months for best quality.

The Science

The reason cooked chicken lasts only a few days in the fridge is due to bacteria such as Salmonella and Listeria, which can grow at temperatures above 40°F (4°C). Once chicken is cooked, these bacteria begin to thrive if not stored properly. Enzymes present in meat can also lead to spoilage over time, even in the cooler temperatures of your fridge. Keeping your cooked chicken in a stable cool environment minimizes these risks.

Signs It's Gone Bad

It’s crucial to check for signs of spoilage before using leftover chicken. Look for:

  • An off or sour smell
  • A slimy or sticky texture
  • A change in color, particularly if it becomes gray or dull
  • Mold or any unexpected growth

If you observe any of these signs, it’s safer to discard the chicken rather than take a chance.

Quick Reference

  • Storage Duration: 3 to 4 days in the fridge
  • Storage Tips: Use airtight containers or wraps, and keep the fridge at or below 40°F
  • Signs of Spoilage: Off smell, slimy texture, discoloration, or mold
  • Action Tip: Toss it after 4 days for safety
