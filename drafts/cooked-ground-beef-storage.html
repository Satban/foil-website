How Long Does Cooked Ground Beef Last?

Cooked ground beef can be safely stored in the fridge for 3-4 days, and it will last 2-6 months in the freezer. So, if you've got leftovers after taco night, you're in good shape for a few days!

The Details

Storing cooked ground beef properly is essential for maintaining its freshness and safety. Keep it in an airtight container or tightly wrap it in aluminum foil or plastic wrap to prevent exposure to air and moisture. The longer you keep your ground beef stored, the greater the risk of spoilage, although freezing can dramatically extend its shelf life.

The Science

Why do these timeframes exist? Factors such as bacteria, enzymes, and oxidation all play a role in food spoilage. Ground beef, specifically, can be a haven for bacteria like Salmonella and Escherichia coli if not handled properly. Keeping your cooked beef below 40°F in the fridge helps prevent bacteria from entering the ‘danger zone’ (40-140°F), where pathogens thrive.

Signs It's Gone Bad

Is that leftover meat still good? Check for these telltale signs of spoilage: an off odor, slimy texture, or any discoloration. If your cooked ground beef smells sour or has a film, it’s best to toss it out. Remember, when in doubt, throw it out!

Quick Reference

  • Fridge: 3-4 days
  • Freezer: 2-6 months
  • Danger Zone: 40-140°F
