How Long Does Steak Last in the Fridge?

Steak typically lasts 3 to 5 days in the fridge when stored properly. To ensure its freshness and safety, it's important to follow specific storage recommendations.

The Details

When you purchase steak, whether it's a luscious ribeye or a lean sirloin, the clock starts ticking as soon as it arrives home. According to the USDA, raw beef can be safely stored in the fridge for 3 to 5 days. If you're not planning to cook it within that timeframe, consider freezing it, which can extend its life to 4 to 12 months depending on the cut.

Make sure to keep your fridge temperature at or below 40°F to effectively slow down bacterial growth. Store the steak in its original packaging or wrap it tightly in plastic wrap or aluminum foil to minimize exposure to air, which can lead to oxidation and spoilage.

The Science

So, why does steak spoil? It all comes down to bacteria and enzymes. As meat sits, bacteria like Salmonella and Escherichia coli can proliferate if not kept at safe temperatures. Enzymatic processes naturally occur in meat, contributing to spoilage and changes in flavor and texture over time. Keeping steak at temperatures above 40°F can push it into the danger zone, where bacteria thrive and multiply rapidly.

Signs It's Gone Bad

Knowing when steak has gone bad can save you from an unpleasant meal—or a trip to the doctor's office. Look for:

  • Color change: Fresh steak should be a bright red or pink; brown or gray spots can indicate spoilage.
  • Texture: If it feels slimy or sticky, toss it.
  • Smell: A sour or off smell is a sure sign that your steak is past its prime.

Quick Reference

  • Fresh steak: 3 to 5 days in the fridge
  • Frozen steak: 4 to 12 months
  • Optimal fridge temperature: ≤ 40°F
  • Danger zone: 40-140°F
