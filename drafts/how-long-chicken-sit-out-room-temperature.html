How Long Can Chicken Sit Out at Room Temperature?

Raw or cooked chicken shouldn't sit out at room temperature for more than two hours to keep it safe to eat. If the room temperature exceeds 90°F, the time drops to just one hour. This guideline is crucial for avoiding foodborne illness.

The Details

When it comes to chicken, safe handling is key. Whether you’re preparing a family dinner or a backyard barbecue, it's essential to store chicken properly. The USDA recommends refrigerating chicken at temperatures below 40°F for optimal preservation. Cooked chicken can be stored for 3-4 days in the fridge, while raw chicken lasts 1-2 days. If you know you won’t use it in that timeframe, freezing is a great option, significantly extending its life.

The Science

Why is it so important to keep chicken out of the danger zone? Bacteria love the temperature range between 40°F and 140°F—this is known as the danger zone. In this range, bacteria like Salmonella and Campylobacter can multiply rapidly, increasing the risk of foodborne illness. The enzymes and microbes present in chicken begin breaking it down even at room temperature, making it important to follow time limits strictly.

Signs It's Gone Bad

To avoid an unpleasant surprise, watch for these signs that chicken has gone bad: foul smell, sticky or slimy texture, or a change in color (grey or green hues). If any of these are present, it's better to err on the side of caution and discard it.

Quick Reference

  • Raw chicken: ≤ 2 hours at room temperature
  • Cooked chicken: ≤ 2 hours at room temperature
  • Store raw chicken in fridge: 1-2 days
  • Store cooked chicken in fridge: 3-4 days
